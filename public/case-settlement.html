Settlement Engine Refactor

The Problem

The original settlement engine evolved organically over time. Business rules accumulated. Execution paths intertwined. Balance adjustments were tightly coupled with side effects. Debugging required tracing through large files with hidden dependencies.

The Risk

In financial systems, subtle ordering mistakes can result in incorrect balances. Race conditions may not appear immediately — but when they do, the cost is high.

The Approach

I redesigned the engine using a builder + execution pattern. Each settlement step became explicit. Execution order became deterministic. Balance updates were isolated. Observability was embedded directly into the workflow.

The Result

The refactored system reduced cognitive load, improved debugging clarity, and eliminated balance inconsistencies caused by sequencing errors. Future workflow extensions became safer and more predictable.

What I Learned

Complexity rarely comes from business logic alone. It comes from hidden coupling and implicit execution paths. Making flows explicit is one of the most powerful architectural decisions.
